# Executive Domain

## Purpose

This document formalizes the executive domain of CommandCore.

It defines the canonical meaning of Jarvis, the Executive Layer, and the executive abstractions that logically follow from the locked blueprint.

## Responsibilities

- Govern executive reasoning across the CommandCore universe.
- Provide briefing, review, delegation, risk escalation, and coordination functions.
- Maintain alignment between company worlds, capabilities, projects, agents, and knowledge.
- Ensure executive decisions operate from structured memory and source records.

## Ownership

The Executive Domain is owned by the Nexus as CommandCore executive headquarters.

Jarvis is the primary executive intelligence role inside this domain.

## Lifecycle

Sprint 1:
- The dashboard is the first form of the Nexus.
- Jarvis exists in naming, architecture, and documentation.
- No fake AI behavior or autonomous executive decisions are implemented.

Future:
1. Executive inputs are collected from search, activity, decisions, next actions, and company state.
2. Jarvis and executive roles synthesize this information into briefs and recommendations.
3. Work is routed to humans, projects, capabilities, and agents.
4. Outcomes are returned to memory and become part of executive context.

## Relationships

- The Nexus contains the Executive Layer.
- Jarvis is Chief Executive Intelligence.
- Hermes, Odysseus, Athena, Sentinel, Forge, Oracle, and Mercury are executive roles.
- The Executive Layer can inspect and coordinate any company, division, department, project, team, agent, or task.
- The Memory Layer depends on search, knowledge records, decisions, and next actions.
- The Approval Layer depends on agent work contracts, approval gates, forbidden actions, and human reviewers.

## Future Extensions

- Morning Brief as a first-class executive object
- Health signals across company worlds
- ExecutiveBoard workflows
- Cross-company portfolio summaries
- Structured escalation patterns
- Executive attention scoring

## Examples

Example 1:
- Jarvis identifies that a project has no next action, cites the source record, and recommends a follow-up.

Example 2:
- Athena reviews blocked operational items across company worlds and surfaces missing runbooks.

Example 3:
- Odysseus promotes a research finding into a capability candidate for executive review.

## Rules

- Jarvis is not merely a chat interface.
- The Executive Layer is an operating model, not mascot branding.
- Sprint 1 does not implement autonomous executive behavior.
- Executive intelligence must cite source records rather than invent context.
- Executive functions must preserve the no-secret rule.

## Canonical Definitions

### Jarvis

Jarvis is Chief Executive Intelligence.

Jarvis is the command layer and executive reasoning surface of CommandCore. Jarvis produces or will eventually produce briefings, summaries, blocker detection, next-action recommendations, work routing, and risk escalation.

### Executive Layer

The Executive Layer is the senior intelligence and coordination layer of CommandCore.

It governs executive reasoning, review, delegation, briefings, security, research, operations, infrastructure, communications, memory, and capability management across the entire universe.

### Executive Intelligence

Executive Intelligence is the function of turning structured operational memory into actionable executive understanding.

It is expressed through briefing, summarization, source-cited recommendation, escalation, coordination, and gap detection.

### Decision Engine

Decision Engine is the canonical name for the executive reasoning function that evaluates context and produces recommended decisions or next actions.

It is derived from the locked Jarvis responsibilities, decision records, and explicit rule that every meaningful item should have a decision or next action.

It is a conceptual domain function, not an implemented service requirement.

### Mission Orchestrator

Mission Orchestrator is the canonical name for the coordination function that aligns executive intent with companies, projects, capabilities, humans, and agents.

It is derived from Jarvis routing work to the appropriate actor and from the future agent work contract.

It is conceptual in Sprint 1 and should not be treated as an autonomous runtime.

### Approval Layer

Approval Layer is the governance boundary that controls what work may proceed, who reviews it, and where human approval is required.

It is derived from approval gates, forbidden actions, human reviewer requirements, permission-aware agents, and safe-failure rules.

### Memory Layer

Memory Layer is the executive memory substrate formed by structured records, search, knowledge retrieval, decision history, next actions, and cross-company knowledge.

In Sprint 1 it is primarily built from the local-first workspace records and universal search.

### Relationship to Nexus

The Nexus is the executive headquarters of CommandCore.

The Executive Layer lives inside the Nexus, and Jarvis is the primary executive intelligence role within that headquarters.

In Sprint 1 the dashboard is the first operational expression of the Nexus.
